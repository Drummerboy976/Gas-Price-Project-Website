Current price information

Price History

Gas prices have been at an all-time high, and that even more true for the state of California. California ranks number one in the nation for highest gas prices. The highest recorded average price hit a staggering $6.43 a gallon for regular, and up to $7.01 for diesel. Currently, the national average is $3.40 while California has to pay a premium at $4.77. These prices hit our wallets hard, especially students and workers that rely on commuting to and from work/school every day.

Cost Associations

Owning a vehicle is not cheap. There are costs that come with owning a car that you might not realize until you have to pay them.

  • Car Insurance: Los Angeles resident's pay an average of $2,276 a year on insurance, which is $365 more than the California average and $681 more than the national average.
  • Maintenance: You can expect to pay an average of $1,282 on routine and unexpected maintenance on your vehicle
  • Registration: Depending on the value of your car, the vehicle license fee can really add to the cost of your registration renewal
  • Car prices: Actually purchasing a vehicle in this current economy is difficult. Though not as bad as peak COVID, dealers are still up-marking the costs of their vehicles which makes just getting a new one that much harder.
